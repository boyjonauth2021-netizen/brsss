School at a Glance

A proud state school serving Mapou and northern Mauritius.

Shri Beekrumsing Ramlallah State Secondary School (BRSSS) is a government boys' secondary school situated on Railway Road, Mapou, in the Riviere du Rempart District of northern Mauritius. The school opened its doors in 2003 and was officially inaugurated on 27 May 2005.

Administered under the Ministry of Education, Tertiary Education, Science and Technology of the Republic of Mauritius, BRSSS offers free secondary education from the Foundation Programme through to Form VI (Grade 13), preparing students for Cambridge School Certificate and Higher School Certificate examinations.

History & Background

Over two decades of growth and achievement.

2000–2002
Construction and Development
The school campus was constructed and developed in Mapou, Riviere du Rempart District, in preparation for its opening to students.
2003
First Students Enrolled
The first batch of Form I students was admitted, marking the beginning of the school's academic life even before the official inauguration.
27 May 2005
Official Inauguration
The school was formally inaugurated by Raouf Bundhun, then Vice-President of the Republic of Mauritius, as Mapou State Secondary School.
14 March 2006
School Renaming Ceremony
Presided over by Dr Navin Ramgoolam, then Prime Minister, the school was renamed Shri Beekrumsing Ramlallah State Secondary School in honour of a towering Mauritian figure.
2008
Top School in the North
BRSSS was ranked among the best schools in northern Mauritius and topped the national Cambridge SC rankings in Design & Technology.
2010
Official Website Launched
The school launched its official website brsss.net, extending its online presence and communication with students, parents and the community.
2019
Cambridge Top 500 Milestone
For the first time in the school's history, a BRSSS student was ranked among the Cambridge Top 500 highest achievers globally for the 2018 academic year.

From Mapou SSS to BRSSS

The 2006 renaming was not merely an administrative act — it was a moment of national recognition for a man who devoted his life to education, journalism, social justice, and the preservation of Mauritian heritage.

Today BRSSS carries that legacy forward, nurturing the next generation of Mauritian students in the spirit of “Enlightenment Through Learning.”

Our Namesake

Honouring Shri Beekrumsing Ramlallah (1915–2000).

Bikramsingh Ramlallah was a teacher, social worker, journalist, politician, minister and national heritage champion whose extraordinary life was inseparable from Mauritius' journey to independence and post-colonial development.

🏫

Educator & Social Worker

Ramlallah began as a school teacher and became an active social worker, joining the Arya Samaj movement and founding the youth movement Sewa Samithi in 1940 to promote community values and service.

📰

Founder of the Mauritius Times

On 14 August 1954, he co-founded the Mauritius Times with Sir Kher Jagatsingh. He served as editor-in-chief for 46 remarkable years, championing press freedom and social progress until his death in 2000.

👨‍⚖️

Politician & Legislator

Elected to the Legislative Assembly in 1959, 1963 and 1967 for Constituency No. 6 (Grand Baie–Poudre D'Or), he served as Parliamentary Secretary and Minister from 1959 to 1976.

🏛

Champion of Aapravasi Ghat

Ramlallah campaigned for the preservation of the historic Aapravasi Ghat in Port Louis, today a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and initiated annual remembrance ceremonies from 1970.

✍️

Press Freedom Defender

In 1984, as founder-chairman of the Mauritius Union of Journalists, he led protests defeating legislation that threatened to silence independent newspapers, securing media freedom for Mauritius.

🏅

Enduring National Legacy

Widely regarded as the “father of Aapravasi Ghat,” his contributions to education, journalism and Mauritian heritage led the Government to rename this school in his honour in 2006.

“He was a man whose pen, voice and conscience shaped the course of Mauritian history — educator, journalist, legislator, and guardian of our national heritage.”
— In memory of Beekrumsing Ramlallah (1915–2000)

Results & Achievements

Milestones that define our school's character.

From national rankings to global recognition, BRSSS students and staff have consistently raised the bar for academic excellence in Mauritius.

2008

Best School in Northern Mauritius

BRSSS was recognised as one of the top-performing schools in the north of the island, a testament to the dedication and hard work of its students and staff.

2008

National Design & Technology Champion

The school topped the national Cambridge SC rankings in Design & Technology, placing BRSSS at the very forefront of technical education across all schools in Mauritius.

2019

Cambridge Top 500 Global Achiever

A BRSSS student was ranked among the Cambridge Top 500 highest-achieving candidates worldwide for the 2018 academic year — a first in the school's history and a landmark for the Mapou community.

Student Life

Beyond the classroom, building character.

BRSSS encourages students to engage in activities that develop creativity, civic responsibility, teamwork and a lifelong commitment to community.

🎬

Student Film Production

BRSSS students produced the short film “Kot Yaya,” released on YouTube, showcasing outstanding creativity, storytelling and collaboration beyond the academic curriculum.

🌿

Environmental Action

Students participated in beach cleanup campaigns at Mon Choisy in partnership with AIESEC and Mission Verte, demonstrating environmental awareness and social responsibility.

🤝

Alumni Association

The BRSSS Alumni Association reconnects former students (“Ex-Mapou Boys”) and builds a collaborative network dedicated to supporting current students and the future of the school.

🏆

Sports & Competitions

The school fields teams in inter-school and district-level sports competitions, fostering healthy competition, physical fitness, sportsmanship and school spirit.
